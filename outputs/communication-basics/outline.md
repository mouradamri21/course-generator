# Communication Basics

## Course Overview
This two-week university-level course provides a foundational understanding of communication principles, theories, and practices. Students will explore the dynamics of verbal, nonverbal, and written communication, as well as interpersonal, group, and digital communication. Through interactive lectures, discussions, and practical exercises, learners will develop essential skills to communicate effectively in academic, professional, and personal contexts.

## Learning Outcomes
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
- Understand and apply core communication theories and models.
- Identify and analyze barriers to effective communication.
- Develop proficiency in verbal, nonverbal, and written communication.
- Enhance interpersonal and group communication skills.
- Adapt communication strategies for diverse audiences and digital platforms.
- Critically evaluate communication practices in real-world scenarios.

---

## Course Structure

### Week 1: Foundations of Communication
**Objective:** Introduce the fundamental concepts, theories, and components of communication.

#### Unit 1: Introduction to Communication
- Definition and importance of communication
- Key elements of the communication process (sender, message, channel, receiver, feedback, noise)
- Types of communication: intrapersonal, interpersonal, group, mass, and digital
- **Activity:** Self-assessment of personal communication styles

#### Unit 2: Communication Theories and Models
- Linear, interactive, and transactional models of communication
- Key theories: Social Penetration Theory, Uncertainty Reduction Theory, and Communication Accommodation Theory
- Application of theories in real-life scenarios
- **Activity:** Case study analysis of a communication breakdown

#### Unit 3: Verbal Communication
- Components of verbal communication: clarity, tone, pace, and vocabulary
- Effective speaking techniques: articulation, pronunciation, and active listening
- Overcoming common verbal communication barriers (e.g., jargon, ambiguity)
- **Activity:** Impromptu speaking exercise with peer feedback

#### Unit 4: Nonverbal Communication
- Types of nonverbal communication: body language, facial expressions, gestures, and proxemics
- The role of nonverbal cues in conveying emotions and intentions
- Cultural differences in nonverbal communication
- **Activity:** Nonverbal communication role-play and analysis

---

### Week 2: Applied Communication Skills
**Objective:** Develop practical communication skills for interpersonal, group, and digital contexts.

#### Unit 1: Interpersonal Communication
- Principles of effective interpersonal communication
- Active listening and empathy in conversations
- Conflict resolution and assertiveness
- **Activity:** Pair discussion on resolving a hypothetical conflict

#### Unit 2: Group and Team Communication
- Dynamics of group communication: roles, norms, and cohesion
- Effective participation in meetings and discussions
- Leadership and facilitation skills
- **Activity:** Group problem-solving task with peer evaluation

#### Unit 3: Written Communication
- Structure and clarity in written communication (emails, reports, essays)
- Tone and style for different audiences
- Common pitfalls in written communication (e.g., passive voice, redundancy)
- **Activity:** Drafting and revising a professional email

#### Unit 4: Digital Communication and Media Literacy
- Best practices for digital communication (email, social media, messaging)
- Ethical considerations in digital communication
- Media literacy: evaluating sources and avoiding misinformation
- **Activity:** Analyzing and critiquing a digital communication example

- Weekly reflection journals (20%)
- Group project: Communication analysis (30%)
- Final assignment: Communication strategy plan (30%)
